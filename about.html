About Drip Irrigation

Drip irrigation is a type of micro-irrigation system that delivers water directly to the roots of plants in small, controlled amounts. In this system, water is distributed through a network of pipes, tubing, and emitters or drippers, which release water slowly and steadily to the soil near the plant roots
Key features of drip irrigation include:

  1. Precise Water Delivery: Drip irrigation delivers water directly to the root zone of plants, inimizing water loss due to evaporation or runoff. This ensures that plants receive the right amount of water they need for optimal growth and development.
  2. Water Conservation: Drip irrigation is highly efficient in water usage compared to traditional irrigation methods like sprinkler systems or flood irrigation. By delivering water directly to the root zone, drip irrigation reduces water wastage and promotes water conservation.
